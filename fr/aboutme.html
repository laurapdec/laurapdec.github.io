I'm a student of aeronautical engineering at École Nationale Supérieure de Mécanique et d'Aérotechnique (ISAE-ENSMA), in an double degree program with Universidade Federal de Uberlândia (UFU) in Brazil.

I hope for a collaborative, challenging and motivating work enviroment. I do not mind of extense work hours and do not expect an easy, instead I wish to encounter hardship through which I can grow. Finally, besides the professional development, I expect to also improve my management and communication skills.

In Brazil, I was in the rocketry team, which was a great experience on teamwork, resilience, and leadership. The team was new, so the university feared any accidents. Therefore they did not permit us to construct the rockets. To earn their trust, we conducted valuable research presented at international congresses. Even though we rarely got positive returns, we kept our spirits up and built a solid theoretical basis where future members could easily access the relevant information.

During this period, I gave a one-week course in Python to raise funds, developed a few codes for future projects, conducted research on neural network prediction for aerodynamic coefficients and made a beginner's manual to rocketry aerodynamics. I also developed leadership skills when I got a promotion as chief of the aerodynamic sector. When I left the team, we had already had our first attempt to launch a rocket, with university permission in an army base nearby. As a result of our resilience, they just won second place in the Latin American Space Challenge (LASC) for the category of 1km apogee with solid propulsion.

I also was a member of the fluid mechanics laboratory at my university in Brazil, where I researched both fluid mechanics and combustion in a project funded by Petrobras through a scholarship. I participated in an article on Physics of Fluids with my advisor in which I developed simple simulations with the new methodology and post-processed the data. Besides that, I made slight improvements in the in-house code Multi-Physics Simulator (MFSim), published articles and presented seminars on my research. This experience was valuable because I learned with hands-on experience collaborative coding, which implicated in developing my communication and organization skills.

Our society has already escaleted fast in our energy demand. I do believe humankind will have a vast future with clean energy we just began exploring. Since the value of a company relies on the value it brings to society, considering the lack or abundance of such in the market. So I intend to acquire a lot of know-how in the energetic industry, because I believe that this will be a high-demand field in which a highly qualified professional can make a real difference that impacts the whole world energetically and enviromentally.

Since I was a kid the space always left me speechless, from looking at the stars to finally understanding the depth and complexity of our universe. Combined with my large interest in STEM, aeronautical and aerospace engineering is a compeling opportunity to make a diference in this field I am very passionate about. In addition, it is one of the most interesting fields to study energetics, a field I am very interested in and allows me to make real improvements in our world.
